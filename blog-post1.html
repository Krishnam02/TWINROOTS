Everything You Need to Know About Onions Before Importing from India

A buyer’s guide for sourcing Indian onions.

Onions are one of the most traded vegetables in the world, used daily in homes, restaurants, and food industries. For buyers, India has become a preferred source due to its scale, diversity, and strong export ecosystem. But before you begin importing, here’s what you need to know to make the most of India’s onion supply.

India’s Role in the Global Onion Trade

India is the second-largest onion producer globally, accounting for about 25% of world output. In 2022–23, the country produced over 31 million metric tons, with exports reaching more than 2.5 million tons. Major producing regions like Maharashtra, Gujarat, and Karnataka are known for high-quality onions that meet global demand.

Onion Varieties to Know

  • Red Onions – Strong flavor, widely exported to Asia and the Middle East.
  • White Onions – Milder taste, ideal for Western markets and processed foods.
  • Yellow Onions – Balanced flavor, popular for bulk exports.
  • Shallots – Smaller, sweeter onions used in gourmet cuisines.

Choosing the right variety is essential depending on your market’s food habits and preferences.

Meeting Global Standards

Onions are perishable and sensitive to handling, making quality control critical. India’s export industry has established strong post-harvest systems including:

  • Grading for size and appearance
  • Proper curing for extended shelf life
  • Export-ready packaging using mesh bags or jute sacks
  • Compliance with international food safety norms

Global Demand and Opportunities

The global onion market is projected to grow steadily, with rising consumption in Asia, Europe, and the Middle East. India remains a reliable exporter due to production scale and competitive pricing.
